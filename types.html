Types of Plastic






Any synthetic or semi-synthetic polymer is known as Plastic. I have noticed the different textures and thickness of plastics I use in my daily life. The plastic I use for grocery shopping is thinner and whiter than the transparent plastic used in bubble wraps

  • Polyethylene terephthalate (PET or PETE) -RIC 1: used for plastic bottles and containers, food jars and microwave safe food containers.
  • High density polyethylene (HDPE)- RIC 2: plastic grocery bags, cosmetic containers, milk jugs, cleaning containers and laundry detergent containers.
  • Polyvinyl chloride (PVC or vinyl) -RIC 3: packing materials, piping and siding, window frames, fencing, decking and railing, shrink-wrap, deli meat packaging and medical product packaging, including blood bags.
  • Low density polyethylene (LDPE) -RIC 4: Manufacturing film , container lids, toys, squeezable bottles and other food containers, newspaper, household garbage bags, adhesives and sealants
  • Polypropylene (PP) -IC 5: yogurt containers, bottle caps, medicine bottles and fibers.
  • Polystyrene (PS)- RIC 6: manufacture egg cartons, plastic cups and plates, medicine bottles and loose fill foam packaging, or “packing peanuts.”
  • Other- RIC 7: used for plastics that cannot be categorized as belonging to one of the six resin types. Some products manufactured with this code include oven-baking bags and custom packaging
